# 5 Signs Your Houston Business Website Needs a Redesign

Your website is your business's most visible employee. It works 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and it's often the first thing potential customers see before deciding to call you or keep scrolling.

But here's the problem: most Houston business owners built their website years ago and haven't touched it since. The web has changed dramatically — Google's ranking algorithms, mobile usage patterns, loading speed expectations, and design standards have all evolved.

If any of these 5 signs sound familiar, your website is probably costing you customers right now.

---

## 1. Your Site Takes More Than 3 Seconds to Load

This is the most expensive problem you can have. **53% of mobile users abandon a site that takes longer than 3 seconds to load.** That's more than half your potential customers gone before they see a single word.

**How to check:** Go to [PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/), enter your website URL, and look at the Performance score. If it's below 70, you have a problem.

**Why it happens:** Old websites built with WordPress themes and dozens of plugins accumulate bloat over time. Unoptimized images, render-blocking scripts, and outdated hosting all slow things down.

**The fix:** A modern website built with Next.js or React loads in under 2 seconds. When I rebuilt [GSM A/C's website](https://www.gsmactx.com/), the Performance score went from poor to 90+ — and their bounce rate dropped significantly.

---

## 2. It Doesn't Look Good on Phones

Pull out your phone right now and look at your website. Is the text readable without zooming? Can you tap buttons without accidentally hitting the wrong one? Does the menu work?

**Over 60% of Google searches in Houston happen on mobile devices.** If your site isn't mobile-friendly, you're invisible to the majority of your potential customers.

**Signs of a mobile problem:**
- Text is tiny and hard to read
- You have to scroll sideways to see content
- Buttons are too small or too close together
- Images overflow the screen
- The navigation menu doesn't work on mobile

**The fix:** Mobile-first design. Every website I build starts with the mobile layout first, then scales up to desktop. Not the other way around. This ensures the experience your Houston customers actually have — on their phones — is perfect.

---

## 3. You Can't Find Yourself on Google

Search for your main service + "Houston" on Google. For example: "HVAC repair Houston" or "medical clinic Houston TX" or "home remodeling Houston."

If you're not on the first page, your website isn't doing its job.

**Why it happens:** Older websites often have:
- No meta titles or descriptions
- No schema markup (LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ)
- No location-specific content
- Slow loading speeds (Google penalizes this)
- No sitemap or robots.txt configuration
- No Google Business Profile connection

**The fix:** Local SEO needs to be built into the website's architecture, not added as an afterthought with a plugin. Proper heading structure, schema markup, Houston-specific content, and technical optimization make the difference.

I built [Clinica Hispana Gessner's website](https://www.clinicagessner.com/) with local SEO built in from day one — targeting both English and Spanish searches in the Houston area.

## 4. Your Website Looks Like It Was Built in 2018

Web design trends change fast. If your site still has:

- **Sliders/carousels** at the top (studies show less than 1% of visitors click past the first slide)
- **Stock photos** that look generic and impersonal
- **Walls of text** with no visual hierarchy
- **No clear call-to-action** telling visitors what to do next
- **A design that looks like a template** because it is one

...customers notice. They may not be able to articulate why, but an outdated website feels untrustworthy. And when you're competing with Houston businesses that have modern, professional sites, that impression matters.

**The fix:** A clean, modern design with clear visual hierarchy, real photos or professional imagery, and strategic calls-to-action. The goal isn't to win design awards — it's to build trust and make it easy for customers to contact you.

## 5. You Can't Update It Yourself

How many times have you wanted to change your phone number, update your hours, or add a new service — and had to email your "web guy" and wait days?

If updating your own website feels impossible, that's a design problem.

**Common complaints I hear from Houston business owners:**
- "My developer disappeared and I can't access anything"
- "WordPress is so confusing I'm afraid to break something"
- "I have to pay $150 every time I need a small text change"
- "I don't even know what hosting company my site is on"

**The fix:** A properly built website should be easy to maintain. I give every client:
- Full ownership of their code and domain
- A client portal to track projects and communicate
- Documentation for basic content updates
- Optional maintenance plans starting at $200/month

You should never be locked out of your own website.

## What a Redesign Actually Involves

A website redesign isn't just making things look prettier. Here's what a proper redesign includes:

1. **Performance audit** — Identify what's slowing your site down
2. **SEO audit** — Find out why Google isn't ranking you
3. **Mobile testing** — Check how the site performs on actual phones
4. **Content review** — Is your copy converting visitors into customers?
5. **Competitive analysis** — What are your Houston competitors doing better?
6. **New design and development** — Built from scratch with modern technology
7. **Content migration** — Your existing content moves to the new site
8. **SEO preservation** — Redirects and optimization so you don't lose existing rankings
