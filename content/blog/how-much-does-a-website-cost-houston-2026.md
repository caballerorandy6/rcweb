## What Affects the Price?

The biggest factors that move the price up or down:

1. **Number of pages** — More pages = more design, content, and development time
2. **Custom functionality** — Contact forms are simple. Booking systems, payment processing, and dashboards add complexity
3. **Content creation** — If you need copywriting and photography, that's additional
4. **Timeline** — Rush jobs cost more. A realistic timeline saves you money
5. **Bilingual content** — English and Spanish websites require proper localization, not just Google Translate

## How to Avoid Overpaying

Here's what I tell every Houston business owner who asks:

**Get multiple quotes.** But compare apples to apples. A $500 Wix site and a $3,000 custom Next.js site are completely different products.

**Ask what technology they're using.** WordPress, Wix, and Squarespace are cheaper upfront but come with monthly fees, plugin vulnerabilities, and slow loading. Custom sites built with modern frameworks like Next.js are faster, more secure, and cheaper to maintain long-term.

**Check their portfolio.** Can they show you real Houston businesses they've worked with? Not just demo projects.

**Ask about ongoing costs.** Some agencies lock you into expensive monthly retainers. My approach: you pay for the build, you own the code. Maintenance starts at $200/month if you want it — but it's optional.
